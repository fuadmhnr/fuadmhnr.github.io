Signal Over Noise

1 January 2024·2 mins

Designing for attention starts with defending your own. This is a short field guide to protect focus without turning into a monk.

The Rule of One Window #

Keep one intentional window open: the one where the work happens. Every extra tab is a tiny promise you will break later.

A Tiny Checklist #

  • What must exist when I stop today?
  • What is the smallest version that proves the idea?
  • What can I delete without guilt?

A Quote That Helps #

If you don’t guard your time, the internet will schedule it for you.

A Small Table #

Signal Noise Replace With
endless tabs false options a short list
alerts fragmented time a single inbox
vague goals drift a clear deliverable

A Compact Timeline #

  1. Morning: produce the core artifact
  2. Midday: tighten and ship the smallest slice
  3. Evening: record what to fix next

A Pull Quote #

Make the work visible, make the scope small, make the finish real.

A Callout #

No tool will rescue a vague goal. Tools amplify clarity or confusion.

An Image #

The Close #

The hardest part of creating is making a clear line. I stop here so tomorrow has a place to begin.
